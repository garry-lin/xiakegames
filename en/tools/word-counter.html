How to use this word counter

  1. Paste text into the box.
  2. Review the live statistics above the text area.
  3. Use keyword frequency to notice repeated terms.
  4. Edit your text and watch the counts update instantly.

Common uses

Writers use word counters to fit article briefs, students use them to check assignment limits, creators use them to prepare captions, and teachers use them to create short classroom writing tasks. Because the counter updates locally in the browser, it is fast enough for drafting and revision. It should not be treated as a legal or academic measurement system because different platforms may count emojis, hyphenated words, and punctuation slightly differently.

Example workflows

For school assignments, paste your draft before submission and compare the word count with the required range. For blog posts, use the reading-time estimate to decide whether the introduction is too long. For product pages, check whether repeated keywords sound natural instead of forced. For social media captions, use the character count before moving text into a platform with stricter limits. These small checks help you edit before publishing instead of discovering problems after the text is live.

Privacy and limitations

The counter runs in the browser and is designed for everyday writing checks. It does not replace official rules from a school, publisher, advertising platform, or legal document system. If a platform has its own word-count rules, use this tool for drafting and then confirm the final number inside that platform. Avoid pasting private student records, passwords, confidential contracts, or payment details.

FAQ

Does this upload my text?
No. The calculation runs in your browser. Avoid pasting sensitive data into any online page.
How is reading time estimated?
The tool uses a simple average reading speed, so it is an estimate rather than a guarantee.
Can I use it on mobile?
Yes. The page is designed for phones, tablets, and desktop browsers.
