Random Name Picker

Paste names line by line and pick one at random. This tool is useful for classroom participation, small giveaways, team warmups, study games, meeting order, and quick decisions when you want a neutral choice.

Fair-use tip

Use one name per line. You can keep names in the list for repeated draws or remove the selected name after each round. For fairness, prepare the list before drawing and avoid editing it mid-round unless everyone agrees.

    Example workflows

    Teachers can paste a class list for discussion turns, warm-up questions, or presentation order. Small teams can use the picker to choose a note taker, decide meeting order, or rotate simple responsibilities. Families and clubs can use it for casual games or small activity choices. When you need multiple winners, draw one name, remove it from the list, and draw again until the list is complete.

    Fairness and privacy notes

    Prepare the list before the draw and let participants know whether selected names will stay in the pool. The tool is designed for casual fairness, not for regulated lotteries, paid raffles, or legal drawings. Names are handled in the browser page, but you should still avoid entering full legal names, student ID numbers, addresses, phone numbers, or other unnecessary personal information.

    Classroom and activity ideas

    Use the picker for reading order, quiz turns, presentation sequence, group discussion starters, chores, game teams, or a quick choice between activity options. For repeated classroom use, keep a clean list in a separate document and paste it at the start of each session. This keeps the draw transparent and avoids accidentally leaving out a participant.

    Tip: test the result with a short sample first, then apply the same format to the full text when it looks correct.

    FAQ

    Is the picker truly random?
    It uses the browser random number generator, which is suitable for casual classroom and activity use.
    Can I use it for official lotteries?
    No. It is intended for casual decisions, lessons, and small activities, not legally regulated drawings.
    Are names uploaded?
    No. The list stays in the page during your browser session.
